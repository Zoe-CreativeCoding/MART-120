A Chilly Autumn Morning

The city spreawled out like the roots of a tree, stretching out in no disernable order. The only constant in the streets is that they all branched off from the castle.


The cyclopean walls of the castle complex rose high above the wattle and daub houses of the poorer class.


The houses were all mismatched, added on to as families grew and spread. Some had swollen to the point that they hovered above the back alleys like a canopy.


It was a chilled autumn morning, steam rising off the sweat soaked backs of horses, mixing with the smoke of dozens of hearths.


There was a strange buzz in the air of anxiety and urgency. That winter promised to be a ruthless one and the days to repare were running out.
